# Editorial Decision Checklist

A structured decision framework for determining whether a development warrants a story, a flag, a trend note, or no action.

---

## Step 1: Identify the development

Before applying any criteria, be clear about what happened:

- What is the specific, discrete thing that occurred? (A vote, a filing, a disclosure, a pattern observed)
- When did it occur? Is it time-sensitive?
- Which entity or entities does it involve?
- What document(s) is this derived from, and what tier are those sources?

If you cannot articulate the development in one or two sentences, it may not be a discrete story candidate yet — it may be a pattern observation (see Trend Note below).

---

## Step 2: Apply the news value test

Score the development against the seven news values. A checkmark does not mean "this is the lead angle" — it means "this factor is present."

| News value | Present? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Timeliness** | ☐ Yes ☐ No | Did this happen recently, or did we just learn of it? |
| **Impact** | ☐ Yes ☐ No | Are identifiable people materially affected? How many? |
| **Proximity** | ☐ Built-in | (Always present in local government coverage) |
| **Controversy** | ☐ Yes ☐ No | Contested vote, public opposition, policy reversal? |
| **Prominence** | ☐ Yes ☐ No | Named elected official, prominent organization, known entity? |
| **Currency** | ☐ Yes ☐ No | Is this connected to an ongoing public conversation? |
| **Oddity** | ☐ Yes ☐ No | Is this unexpected, anomalous, or inconsistent with pattern? |

**Score**: 0-1 criteria → Kill or Trend Note. 2 criteria → Flag. 3+ criteria → Pursue.

---

## Step 3: Apply the significance test

Beyond news values, ask:

**Dollar threshold**: Does this involve public money above a material threshold?
- For most local agencies: any single expenditure, contract, or budget change above $100,000 warrants attention
- Adjust threshold for agency size: a $100K contract is material for a small district; a $1M threshold may be more appropriate for a large city

**Decision consequences**: Does this change something in the world for identifiable people?
- A vote that changes a service, a rate, a right, or a physical space: Yes
- A procedural vote that keeps the status quo: Usually no

**Documentary basis**: Is this finding based on a Tier 1 or Tier 2 source?
- Tier 1 (official record): full confidence in stating the fact; the source is citable
- Tier 2 (institutional publication): attributable with source notation
- Tier 3-4: requires corroboration before meeting the threshold

**Corroboration level**: Is the key claim verified or single-source?
- Verified (appears in multiple independent documents): meets the threshold
- Single-source assertion: may not yet meet the threshold; flag for additional corroboration

---

## Step 4: Apply the kill/flag/pursue decision

Based on Steps 2 and 3:

### PURSUE — story ready for drafting

All of the following must be true:
- The development is discrete and articulable
- It scores on 3+ news value criteria OR scores on 2 criteria with strong dollar/impact significance
- The key claims are documented (Tier 1 or Tier 2 source, corroborated)
- The story can be drafted now with available information

Action: Create a story flag artifact with source citations, key claims, and the primary news angle.

---

### FLAG — warrants editorial attention, more needed

All of the following must be true:
- The development is significant (2+ news value criteria or high dollar/impact significance)
- But: a key element is missing (outcome pending, document not yet available, corroboration needed)
- Or: the story requires cross-beat context that is not yet assembled

Action: Create a flag artifact noting: what is known, what is missing, what would trigger the pursue decision.

Monitor list: add to the monitoring queue with the trigger condition for re-evaluation.

---

### TREND NOTE — pattern accumulating, not yet a story

All of the following must be true:
- Multiple data points suggest a pattern
- No single data point is yet sufficient to pursue
- The pattern, if confirmed, would be significant (3+ news value criteria, likely)

Action: Create a trend note artifact logging: the data points observed, the emerging pattern, the threshold that would trigger a flag or pursue decision.

---

### KILL — no editorial action needed

Any of the following:
- Scores on 0-1 news value criteria with no exceptional dollar/impact significance
- Is a routine procedural action (consent approval, minutes approval, adjournment)
- Is a continuation of a previously documented, stable situation
- The source tier is too low (Tier 3-4 without corroboration) and pursuit of corroboration is not warranted

Action: Archive; no editorial artifact created. Document the kill decision if a flag was under consideration (so the decision is auditable).

---

## Step 5: Determine priority if Pursue or Flag

Not all stories and flags are equally urgent. Prioritize by:

1. **Imminent deadline**: a vote is happening today or tomorrow; a filing deadline is approaching
2. **High stakes**: large dollar amount, many people affected, significant policy change
3. **Scarcity**: this development is not available anywhere else; coverage urgency is high
4. **Cross-beat significance**: the development connects to multiple beats simultaneously

---

## Reference: flag categories for government coverage

When creating a flag artifact, categorize it for routing:

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| `vote-contested` | A split vote (especially 4-3, 3-2) on a significant item |
| `contract-unusual` | Sole-source, no-bid, or unusual waiver in a contract award |
| `budget-significant` | Material budget change, reserve draw, or structural imbalance |
| `disclosure-new` | A new fact revealed in a document (liability, settlement, finding) |
| `entity-crossbeat` | An entity appearing in an unexpected context or across multiple beats |
| `pattern-emerging` | A recurring pattern across documents or time periods |
| `absence-significant` | A required document that has not appeared |
| `reversal` | A prior decision being amended, rescinded, or contradicted |
