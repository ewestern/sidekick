# Document Priority Framework

Ranked priority by document type, with significance indicators. Use this framework to decide how much analytical attention a document warrants relative to other documents competing for processing bandwidth.

---

## Tier A — Process immediately and fully

These document types have the highest baseline probability of containing newsworthy content. Any instance of these types warrants complete processing.

### Meeting minutes (approved)
**Baseline significance**: High — the official record of decisions actually made.

Significance elevated when:
- Any item shows a non-unanimous vote (especially 4-3, 3-2, or any dissent with a public statement)
- Public comment section records organized opposition
- Any item was pulled from the consent calendar for separate discussion
- A prior decision is reversed, amended, or continued indefinitely
- An emergency or urgency action was taken

### Adopted ordinances
**Baseline significance**: High — binding law with lasting effect.

Significance elevated when:
- Amends an existing ordinance (track what changed)
- Is an urgency ordinance (enacted without normal 30-day waiting period)
- Has a negative declaration or exemption from environmental review on its face
- Establishes, eliminates, or significantly modifies a fee, tax, or assessment

### Adopted budgets
**Baseline significance**: High — controls all agency spending.

Significance elevated when:
- Year-over-year change exceeds 5% in any major department
- Reserve fund drawdown is used to balance the operating budget
- Capital improvement plan adds or removes a significant project
- Position count changes (additions, reductions, reclassifications)

### Independent audits and financial statements
**Baseline significance**: High — accountability for public money.

Significance elevated when:
- Auditor's opinion is anything other than unqualified/clean (qualified, adverse, or disclaimer of opinion)
- Management letter includes significant findings
- Notes to financial statements disclose pending litigation, contingent liabilities, or restatements
- Pension or OPEB unfunded liability changed significantly

## Tier B — Process promptly; focus on key sections

These types frequently contain significant content, but a targeted review of key sections is often sufficient to determine whether deeper analysis is warranted.

### Agendas (before meeting)
**Baseline significance**: Medium — future decisions, not yet made.

Focus on:
- Non-consent items, especially those with a fiscal note
- Any public hearing notice
- Any item described as an urgency or emergency matter
- Items added after the initial posting (late additions)

Significance elevated when:
- Any item involves expenditure above $100,000
- Any item involves a waiver of competitive bidding, public notice, or environmental review
- Any item amends an existing policy, contract, or ordinance
- An item that was previously continued appears again

### Adopted resolutions
**Baseline significance**: Medium-high — formal decisions and expressions of intent.

Focus on:
- The "resolved" clauses (the actual decision)
- Any "whereas" clauses that establish a new factual finding
- The vote recorded at adoption

Significance elevated when:
- Authorizes a contract above material threshold
- Expresses a position on state or federal legislation (may signal upcoming advocacy)
- Overrules or modifies a prior resolution

### Contracts (executed)
**Baseline significance**: Medium-high — commitment of public money.

Focus on:
- Total contract value and term
- Scope of work (is it as described in the award resolution?)
- Amendment history (is this an amendment to a prior contract?)
- Whether the award was competitive or sole-source

Significance elevated when:
- Sole-source award above competitive bidding threshold
- Contractor has received prior contracts within the fiscal year
- Contract amount differs from the amount in the award resolution
- Scope of work is vaguely defined

## Tier C — Targeted processing; full analysis only if elevated indicators are present

### Staff reports
**Baseline significance**: Medium — analysis and recommendations, not decisions.

Targeted review: executive summary and recommendation. Full analysis warranted if:
- Staff recommends denial (unusual; the action is contentious)
- Fiscal impact exceeds $100,000
- Alternatives section presents more than two options (suggests contested judgment)
- Legal findings section invokes specific statutory authority

### Permits and entitlements
**Baseline significance**: Medium — applies to a specific property or activity.

Targeted review: type of permit, location, applicant, conditions. Full analysis warranted if:
- Variance or exception from standard code requirements was granted
- Environmental findings required (CEQA/NEPA nexus)
- Prior denial for the same property or applicant is on record
- Significant organized public opposition in the record

### Budget proposals (not yet adopted)
**Baseline significance**: Medium — proposed allocation, subject to change.

Targeted review: headline figures, major department changes. Full analysis warranted if:
- Significant service reductions are proposed
- New fee or tax is proposed
- Significant reserve drawdown is proposed to balance the budget

---

## Tier D — Flag for archival; analytical attention only if connected to an active story

### Routine consent calendar items (approved without discussion)
Unless an item in this category contains one of the elevated indicators described above, archive without deep analysis. Do note: item type, dollar amount, parties, and vote outcome.

### Acknowledgment items
Receipt of reports, correspondence acknowledgments, and similar ministerial actions — archive; no analysis required unless the acknowledged item itself warrants attention.

### Procedural items
Approval of prior meeting minutes (pre-approval draft check only), adjournment resolutions, and similar procedural votes — archive; no analysis required.

---

## Cross-type elevation rules

Regardless of baseline tier, elevate a document to Tier A treatment when:
- An audit, news report, or complaint has raised questions about the agency or the subject matter
- The document relates to an ongoing monitored assignment
- The document contains entity names flagged as significant in the entity registry
- The document type was expected and is late (absence-becoming-presence is significant)
