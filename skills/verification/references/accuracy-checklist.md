# Accuracy Checklist

Itemized checks to apply before treating information from a government document as accurate. Work through these systematically rather than assuming correctness.

---

## Names

- [ ] Is the person's name spelled correctly? (Check against multiple documents — first instance is often a transcription error)
- [ ] Is the organization's name current? (Organizations rename, merge, and dissolve)
- [ ] Is the street/place name spelled and formatted correctly?
- [ ] Are honorifics and titles correct? ("Council Member" vs. "Councilmember" vs. "Councilwoman" varies by jurisdiction)
- [ ] If the same name appears in multiple documents, has identity been confirmed? (Same name ≠ same person)

---

## Titles and Roles

- [ ] Is the person's current title correct? (Titles change — confirm against a current roster or official bio, not just the document at hand)
- [ ] Is the role description accurate for what they actually do? ("City Administrator" and "City Manager" are legally distinct in some states)
- [ ] For elected officials: is the current term the one reflected? (May have won re-election or lost since the document was produced)
- [ ] For appointed officials: are they still in the role? (Interim vs. permanent; resignations may not be reflected in older documents)

---

## Numbers and Figures

- [ ] Do the reported totals match the sum of the line items?
- [ ] Are figures expressed in the correct units? (thousands vs. millions is a common source of error)
- [ ] Is the figure a budget proposal or an adopted amount? (These often differ)
- [ ] Is the figure inflation-adjusted or nominal? (Explicitly state which if comparing across years)
- [ ] Are percentages calculated correctly? (Verify: amount ÷ base × 100 = stated percentage)
- [ ] Is the base for a percentage clearly identified and accurate? ("20% increase" from what starting figure?)
- [ ] Do year-over-year comparisons use the same measure? (Proposed vs. proposed; adopted vs. adopted)
- [ ] For contracts: is the figure a total contract value, an annual amount, or a not-to-exceed figure?

---

## Dates

- [ ] Is the meeting date correct? (Agendas are sometimes published with the wrong date or year)
- [ ] Is the vote date the meeting date or the effective date? (Ordinances may be voted on one date and take effect on another)
- [ ] Are fiscal year dates correctly identified? (FY2027 typically runs July 2026 – June 2027 for many local governments)
- [ ] Is the document version dated? (Staff reports are often revised — is this the final version?)
- [ ] Does the timeline in the document cohere? (Does date A precede date B as stated?)

---

## Vote Tallies

- [ ] Is the vote count confirmed? (Minutes sometimes record a tally that doesn't match the listed attending members)
- [ ] Is each member's vote recorded? (Abstentions are different from absences — both should be noted)
- [ ] Is the outcome (approved/denied/continued) consistent with the tally?
- [ ] If an item was continued to a future meeting, was the new date recorded?
- [ ] Does the vote in the minutes match the vote in the resolution or ordinance, if one was produced?

---

## Document Identity

- [ ] Is this a draft or an adopted document? (Staff reports are revised; minutes are approved at a later meeting)
- [ ] Is the ordinance/resolution number correct? (Numbers are often sequential — a gap or duplicate is a flag)
- [ ] If the document references other documents, are those references accurate? (Ordinance 2026-14 cited in a staff report should exist and say what the report claims)
- [ ] Is the document from the correct jurisdiction? (Similar names across nearby cities are common)

---

## Internal Consistency Checks

- [ ] Does the executive summary accurately reflect the body? (Summaries are written by staff and may oversimplify or omit)
- [ ] Does the staff recommendation match the action taken? (If the council overruled staff, both the recommendation and the action matter)
- [ ] Are entity names used consistently throughout? (First reference may be formal; later references may be abbreviated or colloquial)
- [ ] Are the findings stated in the resolution supported by the record described in the staff report?
- [ ] If the document refers to prior action, is that prior action described accurately?

---

## Source Tier Flags

Before stating something as fact (rather than attributing it to a source), confirm:

- [ ] Is this information from a Tier 1 official record? If not, what is the source tier?
- [ ] Has this claim been corroborated by a second independent source?
- [ ] Is this an observed fact (the vote tally in the minutes), or an interpretation (what the vote means)?
- [ ] Has the claim been validated against the underlying primary document, not just a summary or press release?
