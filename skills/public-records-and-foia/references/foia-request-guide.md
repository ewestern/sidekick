# FOIA / Public Records Request Guide

Practical guidance on crafting effective requests and following up on them.

---

## Before filing a request

**Check whether the record is already available.** Many documents that require requests are not posted online but can be obtained more quickly by calling the records office or checking at the counter. A request formalizes the process; sometimes a phone call or email is faster.

**Identify the correct agency.** A contract between the city and a county agency may be held by both. A grant may be in federal databases. File with the agency most likely to have the most complete version.

**Identify the correct law.** For state and local agencies: the applicable state public records law. For federal agencies: the federal FOIA (5 U.S.C. § 552). Cite the applicable law in your request — it signals you know your rights and may improve response time.

**Know the deadlines.** State laws specify when agencies must acknowledge a request and when they must produce records. Common timelines:
- California CPRA: 10 days to acknowledge; production "without undue delay" (typically 10-30 days)
- Texas PIA: 10 business days to respond
- Florida sunshine law: "reasonable time" (often within 5-7 business days for simple requests)
- Federal FOIA: 20 business days to respond

---

## Drafting the request

**Be specific.** A broad request generates a slow, expensive, and incomplete response. Specificity reduces search time, reduces cost, and increases the chance of getting exactly what is needed.

**Identify the records by type, topic, and date range.** Good request language:
> "I request all records, including but not limited to contracts, invoices, correspondence, and internal memoranda, related to Contract No. 2026-112 with ABC Consulting LLC, covering the period from January 1, 2025 through the date of this request."

**Name specific documents if known.** If you know a document exists (because it was referenced in another record), name it:
> "I request the staff report prepared for the March 11, 2026 city council meeting regarding Agenda Item 7B (pool management contract)."

**Specify format.** Request electronic records in their native format (Excel, not PDF; email with metadata, not printed). Most state laws allow requesters to specify format:
> "I request that records be produced in electronic format. For emails and correspondence, I request native format files that preserve metadata."

**Assert fee waiver or reduction.** If you are a journalist or media organization, assert your status and request a fee waiver. Most states have provisions for media fee waivers or reductions:
> "As a journalist engaged in gathering news for public dissemination, I request a fee waiver. If any fees are anticipated to exceed $[threshold], please notify me before incurring costs."

**Request notification before withholding.** Ask the agency to notify you before withholding any records, stating the specific exemption claimed:
> "If any responsive records are withheld in whole or in part, please identify each withheld record and state the specific statutory exemption under [state law] that is claimed."

## Sample request template

```
[Date]
[Agency Records Officer / City Clerk / FOIA Officer]
[Agency name and address]

RE: Public Records Request under [applicable law]

Dear Records Officer:

Pursuant to [California Public Records Act / Texas Public Information Act / etc.], I request the following public records:

[Specific description of records — be as specific as possible]

Relevant time period: [start date] through [end date, or "the date of this request"]

I request that records be produced in electronic format where available.

As [a journalist / a member of the media / etc.], I request a fee waiver. If fees are expected to exceed $[amount], please notify me before incurring any costs.

If any records are withheld, please identify each withheld document and specify the statutory exemption claimed.

I look forward to your response within the [10/20]-day period required by [applicable law]. Please confirm receipt of this request.

Sincerely,
[Name, title, organization, contact information]
```

## Following up

**Log every step.** Record the date the request was submitted, the date of any acknowledgment, any extension claimed, and the date of production or denial. This log is the basis for any appeal or complaint.

**Follow up when the deadline passes.** If the agency has not responded within the required time, contact the records officer by phone and email. Note the contact in your log.

**Appeal denials.** Most public records laws provide an appeals process:
- California: administrative appeal to the agency, then lawsuit in superior court
- Texas: opinion request to the Office of the Attorney General
- Federal FOIA: administrative appeal within the agency, then federal court

**Challenge over-broad exemption claims.** If an agency withholds records citing an exemption, ask them to identify the specific statutory provision and explain how it applies. Over-broad withholding is common; a specific challenge often produces partial production.

**Request a Vaughn index (federal FOIA).** For federal requests, if records are withheld, request a Vaughn index — a document-by-document description of each withheld record and the specific exemption claimed. This enables meaningful assessment of whether the withholding is justified.

## Common obstacles and responses

| Obstacle | Response |
|---|---|
| "We don't have records responsive to your request" | Ask them to confirm in writing; ask what records they do have on the topic |
| "This will take 6 months" | Ask for a written timeline; cite the applicable deadline; offer to narrow the request |
| "This request is too burdensome" | Narrow the request; offer to prioritize; ask what portion could be produced sooner |
| "Those records are exempt" | Ask for the specific statutory basis; ask for non-exempt portions to be produced |
| "We need more information to process your request" | Provide what is needed; document the correspondence |
| No response at all | Follow up in writing; file a complaint with the applicable oversight body (state AG, etc.) |

## Tracking pending requests

Maintain a request log with:
- Request date and submission method
- Agency contacted and records officer name
- Specific records requested
- Applicable deadline (calculated from submission date)
- Acknowledgment received (date)
- Any extension claimed (date, basis, new deadline)
- Production date and contents
- Any withholding (record description, exemption claimed)
- Appeal filed (if any)

For investigations involving multiple requests to multiple agencies, this log is essential for tracking what has and has not been received.
